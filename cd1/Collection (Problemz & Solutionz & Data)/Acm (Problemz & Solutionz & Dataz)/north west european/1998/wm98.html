The Problem

For each possible match A vs. B between these 16 nations, you are given the probability that team A wins against B. This (together with the tournament mode displayed above) is sufficient to compute the probability that a given nation wins the World Cup. For example, if Germany wins against Belgium with a probability of 80%, England against Croatia with a probability of 60%, Germany against England with a probability of 70% and Germany against Croatia with a probability of 90%, then the probability that Germany reaches the semi-finals is 80% * (70% * 60% + 90% * 40%) = 62.4%.

Your task is to write a program that computes the chances of the 16 nations to become the World Champion '98. Your program has to regard several cases because in furture team coaches will use it to compute whether to qualify as first or second team of a group. Also opinions about the relative strength of teams varies significantly between different experts.

The Input

The first line of input gives the number of cases. For each case, the first 16 lines give the names of the 16 countries, from top to bottom according to the picture given above. Next, a 16 x 16 integer matrix P is specified, where element pi,j gives the probability in percent that country #i defeats country #j in a direct match, e.g. p1, 13 = 64 would mean that in a match between Brazil and Germany, Brazil wins with a probability of 64%. Note that matches may not end in a draw, i.e. For all i, j: pi,j + pj,i = 100

The Output

For each case output one line case #: followed by 16 lines of the form XXXXXXXXXX p=Y.YY%, where XXXXXXXXXX is the country's name, left-justified in a field of 10 characters, and Y.YY is their chance in percent to win the cup, rounded to two decimal places. Use the same order of countries as given in the input file.
